Notes

Opening (first 5 seconds):

"Hey, is this [Owner Name]? … Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] — I'm actually calling because I already built you a website. I was working on a few landscaping companies out here in Orange County and I went ahead and put one together for you — I just wanted to show you what it looks like."

If they ask "who is this / what company?":

"Yeah, my name's [Name], I'm a web designer — I wasn't sure if you'd seen it yet so I wanted to reach out before I moved on to the next company. Can I send you the link real quick?"

If interested:

"Perfect. So I built it to actually convert — not just look nice. It's got your services, a contact form, click-to-call, and it's optimized for people searching 'landscaper near me' in OC. I can walk you through it on a quick 15-minute call — when's a good time this week?"

Common objection — "I already have a website":

"Totally, most companies do — but a lot of them aren't actually bringing in leads. This one is built specifically to convert OC homeowners searching for landscapers. Can I just send you the link so you can compare? Takes 30 seconds."

Common objection — "How much does it cost?":

"I don't charge until we actually talk and you love it. Let me send you the link first — if it's not better than what you have, you just say no and we both move on. Fair?"

Close:

"I'll shoot you the link right now — what's the best email? And if you want to hop on a call, I'm open [Tuesday / Thursday] this week."

When someone else answers:

"Hey, is [Owner Name] around? … Actually, I'm a web designer — I put together a site for [Company Name] and I wanted to get their eyes on it before I moved on. Who handles that stuff there?"

If gatekeeper pushes back:

"Totally understand — I'm not selling anything yet, I literally built something and I just want to show it to whoever makes those decisions. Is there a better time to catch [Name]?"

Get the owner's direct number:

"What's the best number to reach them on? Or an email? I'll keep it short, I promise — it's just a link."

If they won't transfer:

"No worries — what's your name? I'll follow up with you. Can I get your email and I'll send over what I built? You can pass it along when you get a chance."

Voicemail script (keep it under 25 seconds):

"Hey [Owner Name], this is [Your Name] — I'm a web designer and I actually built a website for [Company Name]. I was doing a few landscaping companies out in OC and I went ahead and put one together for you. I just want to send you the link — give me a call back at [Your Number] or I'll try you again. Talk soon."

Key rules:

• Never say "I'm calling about a website" — say you already built one
• Speak at normal pace, sound casual — not scripted
• Leave your number clearly, once
• Never leave more than 2 voicemails per prospect
